During the pre-colonial period, Philippines had its own style of clothing from both men and women which showed the original and traditional fashion in this period.

MAGINOO

Maginoo usually carry the title of Lakan, Datu and Raja, This was the leaders who received respect and services from the lower ranks and their obligation was to protect his subjects and aid them with their necessities.

MAHARLIKA

Maharlika, which connotes to freedom, liberty and nobility composed prominently the family of the Maginoo. This group did not pay taxes but rendered services to the Maginoo.

TIMAGUA

Timagua, are the free but ordinary people. They were people who worked in the fields or fished in the sea or lake for living.

ALIPIN

Alipin, are the ones who are captured in battle, inheritance, by purchase, failure to pay his debt or punished for a crime committed. There are two types of Alipin: first is the Namamahay, they are the ones who could be inherited but not sold, had his own family and helped his master’s field and served his visitors. Second is the Saguiguilid, which are the ones who was entirely disposable, had no property and could not marry without their master’s approval.
