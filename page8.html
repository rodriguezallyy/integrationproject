PRE-COLONIAL ERA: SYSTEM OF HANDWRITING

Filipinos do have their own alphabet and writing system, Baybayin,as well as they definitely know how to read and writes even before the Spaniards came to colonized them.
PreviousNext

They have their own method of writing which they use leaves, bamboos, trunk’s skin and other tools that is sharp to be able to write and read well. Nowadays, Filipinos writing and reading method is from left to right to not be confused but during this period, Filipinos write from top to bottom and read it from left to right. Another one, they also have Baybayin or what we called alphabet now which script is very different in other countries such as India, China, and Japan. This information was according to the first Spanish missionaries who came in the Philippines, Fr. Pedro Chirino.

Thus, this system of writing came from the alphabet of Sumatra. The Filipinos in each region and race have different writing form and dialect. The Visayan, Ilocano, Tagalog and some ethnic groups have an alphabet or so called syllabary that are composed of 17 letters, 14 consonant letters and 3 vowel letters. Meanwhile, the Muslim has a dialect called ‘kirim’ of Mindanao and ‘jiwi’ of the Tausug which they are still using until this modern day.
