PRE-COLONIAL ERA: ECONOMIC LIFE

During the early period, ancient Filipinos were composed of different groups that came from different part of Asia. With different groups they have their own community, beliefs, lifestyle, and source of living. They were situated along the rivers, streams, seashores, fertile land areas and even in caves. With this, the source of living and the place where they live and work is a great proof that Philippines is very rich in resources for a bountiful living.

AGRICULTURE AND FARMING

Agriculture is their main source of livelihood. For those Filipinos living in land areas are the ones who cultivate the land and are definitely good in farming and were proven in this period as they have many agricultural foodstuffs such as rice, banana, coconut, sugar cane, oranges, hemp, cotton, and many species of fruits and vegetables. This type of living was done in two ways which are the kaingin system (slash and burn) and tillage.

The daily fare consisted of rice and boiled fish, or sometimes carabao or wild buffalo meat, pork or venison. Thus, the fermented sap of palm trees and they drank it as liquor is called tuba. And also, this type of work was done in one place to another as Filipinos will move to another place with less grass, fine soil, and abundant of trees where they can start farming again as they no longer use the previous place after harvesting.

LIVESTOCK

Filipinos has a way of living in raising different animals that will benefited them and help them in working and these are pigs, chickens, goats, carabaos, and small native horses

FISHING

It is a thriving industry and this type of living was done by Filipino who live near the rivers, lakes, and any other body of water. They have various tools that they used in fishing and these are nets, bow and arrow, wicker basket, spear, hook and lines, corals and fish poisons. Thus, they will do the fishing and catch different kind of fishes as well as pearls and shells. 

MINING

Mining is also one of the source of living of the early Filipinos before the coming of the Spaniards and they mined different kinds of gold in different parts of the archipelago and were traded throughout the country as well as in other countries.  

LUMBERING AND SHIP BUILDING

It is a flourishing industry which shows that Filipinos are not just good in common work to live their life but they are also skilled in building ocean-going vessels. They built all kinds of boats and ships which later on called banca, lapis, balangay, caracoa, vinta, virey, and prau by the Spaniards. 

WEAVING

It is a type of home industry that the work was done by women. In weaving the clothe they used different tools and materials to create a well done clothes to everybody and these are crude wooden looms, textiles such as sinamay from hemp, medrinaque from banana, cotton, linen, and silk. 

TRADING

This was conducted between or among the barangays or islands that every individual lived in. Filipinos are the just the one who was doing this type of work but also other countries were in the trade world too such as Siam, Japan, China, Cambodia, Sumatra, Borneo, Java, and other islands of old Malaysia. This trading did not use any currency but conducted trade through barter.
