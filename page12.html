PRE-COLONIAL ERA: CLOTHING

During the pre-colonial period, Philippines had its own style of clothing from both men and women which showed the original and traditional fashion in this period.
PreviousNext

From men, wearing a bahag which also translated into English as ‘G-string’,cangan, and putong was their normal clothing. The bahag is a piece of cloth that has almost a meter wide and four to five meters in length that is used to wrapped around the bottom part of their body which is the legs and hips. On the upper part, cangan was used which is a short collarless sleeved jacket that is 19 inches broad and 6 yards long. The color in cangan indicates the rank of every Filipino- red for the chief (a person on the top) and black or blue for the commoners (the bottom people). And also, men have putong which is a turban that was wore on their head that also indicates the social status or achievements of an individual wearing it.

On the other hand, women’s clothes are saya/camisa, baro/patadyong, and tapis. The saya is a tube skirts that is loose and wrap around the waist of a women while the baro is a blouse with big sleeves and shawl collar. Last is the tapis which is a piece of cloth that has a color of red or white that women wore on top of their skirt. Thus, this clothing was made by having a combination of element of the pre-colonial native Filipino and colonial Spanish clothing style.

In addition, the clothing of the Filipinos in this period is traditional while being a partial nudity which is common and does not show any malice. Other than their clothes, they also have different accessories or jewelries and having a huge amount of gold that they are wearing to beautify their bodies as well as different style of clothing and design based on the occasions that they have such as headdresses, blouses, tunics, scarves or ankle-length robes, potong, kalombiga, a ya bun, and many more. Aside from that, their clothing was good and considered as high quality as it made from local and imported materials such as abaca, cotton and silk.
