SPANISH ERA:
SOCIAL STRATIFICATION

Filipinos of Spanish descent currently constitute the great majority of both the upper and middle classes. Most elite Filipino family dynasties, political families and the elite clans are of Spanish origin while a number of Filipinos of Spanish descent are found in the lower classes.

During the Spanish Colonial Period, large numbers of Spaniards settled in the Americas, which resulted in widespread miscegenation between them, indigenous women and enslaved African women. The Spanish authorities developed and established a highly complex caste system based on a racial hierarchy of Spanish descent, which later became associated with whiteness.

  • PENINSULARES- Spaniards in the Philippines, born in Spain
  • INSULARES- Spaniards born in the Philippines ; experienced inferiority and discrimination from Peninsulares 
  • CREOLES- offspring of Spaniard and Indio
  • SANGLEYES- Chinese traders
  • INDIO- Filipinos
