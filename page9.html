PRE-COLONIAL ERA: DIVINATION AND MAGIC CHARMS

In the pre-colonial period in the Philippines, Filipinos also are quite superstitious as they believe in divination or augury and magic charms.
PreviousNext

Filipinos is a group of people believing in things that is out of this world or things that may or may not happen, in short having a superstitious belief. 

One of this are the divination and magic charms that what seems like just happen in those fairytale books that are parents are reading when we are a child. With this, Filipinos then and now are similar with each other as this belief that Filipinos have are still intact in their way of life as it has a connection in their daily lives.

AUGURY is a sign of something is coming or what will happen in the future which is also called as omen. They have different signs that they believe is good or bad omen depending on the circumstances or situations such as the flight of birds, the singing of lizards, the barking of dogs, and so on. They also consulted with the PANGATAUHAN or what we called soothsayer which is a person who people believe is able to predict what will happen in the future, to tell their fortunes which is the charm or luck that they will have in life.

Aside from that, Filipinos also believe that there are the existence of aswang, mangkukulam, manggagaway, tiyanak, tikbalang, and many other supernatural entities and spirits that everyone’s afraid of.

The AMULETS AND CHARMS were also used by the ancients to fight those supernatural creatures as well as to different desires that each of an individual has and these amulets and charms are anting-anting, gayuma, odom or tagabulag, wiga or sagabe, and tagahupa.
